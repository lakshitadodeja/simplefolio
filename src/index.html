Language and Vision Conditioned Imitation Learning

Imitation Learning is an LfD paradigm that does away with the design of a sophisticated reward function. This works aimed to make the process of Imitation Learning more efficient by conditioning it over language and visual inputs along with guidance. I developed a new model that takes the vision inputs, natural language instructions and guidance and predicts the next action the robot should take to complete the tasks. We used BABY AI as the domain for our study. The addition of guidance to our model results in significant improvements. This is especially apparent in the harder GoToSeq level. The model also performed better than the baseline on the GoToSeq level. We devised a new method to generate natural language guidance automatically based on BabyAI's BOT model. This can be used for future experiments on vision and language navigation in BabyAI without depending on humans for generating guidance. We also introduce an even harder variant of GoToSeq level that involves repeated iterations of the same mission. This level challenges the agent to remember the number of iterations of the mission completed based on the mission command.

Predicting Effective Arguments

Due to resource constraints and limitations especially in underrepresented communities, teachers’ ability to issue writing tasks and feedback to students are limited. We trained a model to classify argumentative elements in student writing as "effective," "adequate," or "ineffective”. Our dataset contained about 36k paragraphs/excerpts extracted from 4191 essays written by U.S students in grades 6-12. Each excerpt is rated as "effective," "adequate," or "ineffective” and it belongs to one of the seven discourse elements - lead, position, claim, counterclaim, rebuttal, evidence and concluding statement. This will enable students get automated guidance and feedback on writing tasks and help them improve their writing skills.

Social Bias Classification

The disconnection of social media posts from real-world interactions, and the anonymity of online forums, have led to pervasive toxicity and have influenced broad cultural shifts. Our objective was to identify social biases in social media content. Instead of identifying posts as generalizable “inflammatory” or “hateful”, we are interested in describing posts to higher resolution. Building upon the literature, we look at the toxicity of a post in three categories – whether the post is objectively offensive, whether the post’s intent was to be offensive, and whether the post targets a group or an individual. We deployed several classification architectures and an ensemble method to classify biases within a dataset of social media posts.

Youtube Quality Estimation over MPTCP

I researched how combining WLAN and LTE, using Multipath Transmission Control Protocol (MP-TCP), could help in increasing the Quality of Experience (QoE) of the users of high-quality video streaming services like YouTube. I built an android application to compare the effectiveness of WLAN, LTE and MP-TCP with the help of MOS (Mean Opinion Score) for a playlist of videos. The MPTCP-based LTE-WLAN integration recorded a MOS score 34.4% better than LTE and 20.4% better than WLAN

Energy Efficient Rekeying in Wireless Sensor and Actor Networks

Simulated an energy-efficient rekeying mechanism for clustered WSAN in Omnet++. The rekeying phase of the network was optimized by introducing the concept of critical and non-critical nodes and increasing the rekeying interval where on-demand rekeying took place only if a critical node was compromised, thus reducing the number of key refresh operations.I compared the proposed scheme with Sequence Based Key Management (SKM) scheme, where only periodic rekeying is done at small intervals. I observed that the number of links rekeyed dropped by 50% in both multi-hop and single-hop networks. The energy consumption of key refresh operations also dropped by 34% in single-hop networks and 10% for multi-hop networks. The network lifetime increased by a maximum of 10%. The report I submitted with my findings, as my final year project, received the highest grade and I graduated as one of the top 10 students in my batch.
